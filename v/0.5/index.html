Description

Digital Construction Occupancy ontology represents those aspects of construction and renovation projects that concern the comfort, safety and health of occupants, including visual and thermal comfort, indoor air quality and building acoustics, as well as related sensor observations.

Overview

Digital Construction Occupancy ontology covers the factors affecting occupants' comfort, including indoor air quality and building acoustics. It is based on the DNA (Drivers, Needs, Actions) model developed in the HIT2GAP project .

The class dicob:OccupancyComfort class represents the Needs of the DNAs, with additional subclasses for dicob:ThermalComfort , dicob:VisualComfort and dicob:AcousticComfort . The comfort profile of the user is characterized by comfort requirements that could be defined by fixed limits expressed in terms of dicob:EnvironmentalPreference over specific environmental properties such as temperature, luminance, or db level.

As part of the occupants’ behaviours analysis, occupancy/presence profiling related parameters is considered at each specific residential unit ( dice:ResidentialUnit ). The dicob:OccupancyProfile class represents the occupancy profiling model over a specific time frame, with the model parameters to be specified through the dicob:occupancyParameters class.
